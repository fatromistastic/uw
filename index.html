1. Beyond what is visible, is what is (2025)

~4.5 mins — Orchestra

Score (PDF) · Recording

This work emerges from an ongoing textual experiment: a digital corpus of ‘visionary and prophetic’ texts spanning millennia and cultures — from the Babylonian Astronomical Diaries and the Book of Traversing Eternity to Nostradamus and Blake. I trained a rudimentary large language model (LLM) on this corpus to become a kind of Post-Information-Age oracle, exploring how a definitionally ‘superhuman’ system might refract the profoundly human impulse toward prophecy. Part of a larger chresmology (a collection of prophecies) for orchestra, each movement responds to the model’s first utterances. This movement further excavates the cultural past through the collision of heavily transformed musical fragments: Barber, Messiaen, Mahler, and even transcriptions of wind chimes haunt its surface.

2. Trinomial Overshot (2025)

~3 mins — Fixed media / electronic [NO SCORE]

Listen on SoundCloud

Trinomial Overshot 11 seeks to forge a new and distinct relationship to “the grid” and to periodicity itself. Inspired by the unusual weaving patterns devised by American weaver Ada Dietz, I wrote an algorithm combining OpenMusic and Common Lisp that generates and allows me to audition stochastic, hierarchical patterns of irregular pulse duration. This piece is built entirely around five such rhythms, each of eleven pulses, colliding with additional aleatoric elements. The title, Trinomial Overshot, refers to Ada Dietz’s own beautiful tapestry of the same name.

3. Prometheus opening credits (re-score)

~5 mins — Film Score [VIDEO ONLY]

This video presents my original re-scoring of the opening sequence from Prometheus, performed by the ORT – Orchestra della Toscana under the direction of Hollywood conductor Pete Anthony, at the Teatro Verdi in Florence. For this score, I received the Best Orchestration award in the Chigiana Film Scoring Program, a course selected and taught by members of the USC Thornton Screen Scoring faculty—including Patrick Kirst and Lawrence Shragge—one of the world’s leading programs for composition in visual media.

For rights reasons, excerpts from the original film appear only briefly in this footage
